What Each Indicator Means
Trend (EMA20/50): Price above both EMAs in the right order = strong bull structure. The most important signal.
MACD Daily: Histogram crossing up = momentum turning bullish. Building = momentum accelerating.
MACD Weekly: Weekly MACD agreeing with daily = higher-conviction setup. Disagreement = caution.
Momentum: Raw 5-day % move. Confirms recent price action is following the signal.
Volume: High volume on bullish days = institutional conviction. Low volume = weak move.
RSI-14: Context-sensitive modifier. Overbought (>70) in a bullish setup = penalty. Oversold (<30) in a bearish setup = penalty. Prevents chasing extended moves.
Debit Spread Rules (Default)
DTE: 21–35 days. Enough time for the thesis without paying for excess theta. Exit early — never hold to expiration.
Long leg: ATM or 1 strike ITM (delta ~0.50–0.70). Responds well to price movement.
Short leg: At or just beyond your price target. $2–5 wide (<$100 stocks) / $5–10 wide (≥$100 stocks).
Exit: Take profit at 65–75% of max value. Cut loss at 50% of premium paid. Exit if 21 EMA breaks or MACD rolls over.
IV Rank (IVR) Guide
Below 30% — LowCheap IV — can go wider for more potential
30–50% — NormalStandard debit spread sizing
50–70% — ElevatedShort leg sells expensive premium — spread costs less
70%+ — HighSpread only. Avoid long options entirely.
